Just Sayin'

I am a female lawyer, class of 1974. In the early 1970s I found it necessary to transfer from my first-year law school in one city to a law school in another city - a difficult situation for the very few female law students at that time. Most transfers, I heard, were for (male) law students in the military. I went to hear a presentation by the then pioneer women's rights lawyer - Ruth Bader Ginsburg. After her inspiring presentation, I went up to thank her and mentioned my situation, asking for advice. This lovely, loving woman offered to write a letter for me to the law school I wanted to transfer to. She did, and I was admitted. Thanks to "my hero," I had a 40-year legal career followed by 6 years in "retirement" of teaching lawyers in and from all over the world. I hope she reads this comment and my thanks to her. I have loved her for all these years from afar and have told this story in tribute to her hundreds of times in the U.S. and abroad. Thank you, Ruth Bader Ginsburg, for my happy and successful life. I have carried your grace with me everywhere and paid forward what you did for me.
